Formal Mentoring Lifecycle

Our clients have diverse needs and include professional firms, universities, non-profits, and corporations. Client needs generally fall into one of the 5 stages of the formal mentoring cycle:

Concept Stage

• Consulting

Valuing Formal Mentoring. Leadership understands the impact mentoring can have on their organization, but needs help creating the structure and accountability of the program.

Creating Stage

• Configure Software

Getting the Structure Right. We help you use mentoring best practices aligned with your program’s goals developed during the concept stage to create an effective program structure.

Pilot Stage

• Success Coaching

Calibrating with Participants. Beginning with a small group helps organizations learn how mentoring fits their culture, exposes mistakes quickly, and highlights successes to champion.

Scale Stage

• Scaling Software

Getting the Message Right. Once the program structure supports success and accountability, it’s time to scale the program and effectively communicate its roll-out to the organization.

Manage Stage

• Analysis Reporting

Making Sure it Happens. You’ve invested time and money, and now need to maximize your ROI by effectively tracking activitiy, holding people accountable, and celebrating successes!
